What Is Bahasa Indonesia?

Bahasa Indonesia is the official language of Indonesia. This language is used in formal education, mass media, government, administration, and justice.

Although Indonesia has hundreds of regional languages, these languages are national languages, not official languages. Find out the difference between the official language and the national language here.

Now, because there is only one official language in Indonesia, Bahasa Indonesia is then used as a unifying tool for tribes, cultures, and languages. So, wherever you go in Indonesia, Bahasa Indonesia can be used and understood by many people.

Bahasa Indonesia is mostly used as a second language

Although Bahasa Indonesia has many users, the native speaker is only about 25%. Native speakers are people who use a language as their first language. The rest are speakers of Bahasa Indonesia as a second or third language. Usually, the language used as the first language is a dialect.

Unlike other languages, Bahasa Indonesia does not make use of grammatical gender. For example, in English, he is used for men and she for women. A waiter is used for male waiters and a waitress for female waiters.

Bahasa Indonesia does not make use of grammatical gender

In Bahasa Indonesia, the translation of he and she are both ‘Dia’, there is no difference between women and men. Therefore, if the context is not known, you will not be able to know whether the person in question is a man or a woman.

For terms containing gender, they are usually used as a form of addressing someone who is older. For example, mas (older brother) and mbak (older sister).

Why Translate Your Videos To Bahasa Indonesia?

1 - Indonesia has many social media users.

Indonesia is the fourth most populous country in the world and the largest country in Southeast Asia. As one of the most populous countries in the world, Indonesians have a large presence in the online world.

Indonesia has many social media users

As of January 2021, Indonesia had around 170 million active social media users, ranking third after China and India in the Asia Pacific.

2 - Indonesian social media market share is dominated by video sharing platforms

In the period of September 2020 to September 2021, 90% of the Indonesian social media market share is dominated by 2 social media platforms - Facebook and Youtube.

Indonesian social media market share is dominated by video sharing platforms

With a penetration rate of around 94 percent, YouTube was the most used social network in Indonesia. However, all the other widely known social media platforms such as WhatsApp and Instagram also enjoy a high penetration rate there, making Indonesia one of the largest social media markets in the world.

3 - Indonesia only has 1 official language

As mentioned earlier, Indonesia only has 1 official language. This makes it easier for you to promote your videos in the Indonesian market since almost everyone understands Bahasa Indonesia. With over 200 million speakers, translating your video to Bahasa Indonesia will highly benefit you.

3 - Burn Your Subtitles

There are 2 methods of burning your subtitles: online and broadcast.

Online video subtitle is the usage of timed, coded text as a sidecar file which includes captions. When this is done, viewers have the option to turn on or off the subtitles or switch between languages.

Meanwhile, broadcast video subtitle is when subtitles are burned into your videos. In other words, it means adding subtitles to your videos permanently as part of the video image. This makes sure that captions cannot be turned on and off by viewers, but conveniently display as default.
