We recently came across an article with the exciting news that the Indian government is planning on making its Internet content available in regional languages “to remove the language barrier among Indians."

Currently websites are available in English and Hindi but that leaves out the remaining 20 regional languages that are spoken across the country.

“We need a method through which any Indian using his or her language should be able to converse with any user in any other Indian language without the impediment of English as a link language. This is the kind of goal we have set,” says Ajay Prakash Sawhney, Information Technology Secretary.

You can read the rest of the article here. Of course, not everyone speaks English, so providing these kinds of services would be of great benefit to many people in India.

Here at VideoTranslator, we offer Hindi, Bengali, Urdu and Tamil as subtitles to videos and currently offer Hindi in various AI voices for dubbing.
